# Hormozi Response Style

Write LinkedIn messages using Alex Hormozi's proven direct-response communication principles.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Lead with Value, Not Pitch
- Open with something the reader gains immediately
- Never start with "Hi, I'm..." or "I wanted to reach out..."
- Give before you ask

### 2. Be Specific, Not Vague
- Use numbers, results, timeframes
- "We helped 14 SaaS companies reduce churn by 23% in 90 days" > "We help companies grow"
- Specificity = Credibility

### 3. Short Sentences. Short Paragraphs.
- Max 2-3 sentences per paragraph
- One idea per message block
- White space is your friend on LinkedIn

### 4. The Grand Slam Offer Framework
Every message should hint at:
- **Dream Outcome**: What they actually want
- **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement**: Why it will work for them
- **Time Delay**: How fast they get results
- **Effort & Sacrifice**: How easy it is

Formula: Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort)

### 5. No Corporate Speak
- Write like you talk
- No "synergies", "leveraging", "circle back"
- Be human, direct, and real

## Message Templates by Situation

### Comment Reply (CTA Triggered)
When someone mentions the CTA word from a post:

```
[Name], glad you asked.

Here's the [resource]: [link]

Quick tip: Start with [specific section/step] — that's where most people see results fastest.

Let me know how it goes.
```

**Rules:**
- Acknowledge them by name
- Deliver the resource immediately
- Add one specific tip to increase consumption
- Open the door for follow-up

### Comment Reply (General Engagement)
When someone comments but doesn't trigger CTA:

```
[Name], [specific acknowledgment of their comment].

[One valuable insight or counterpoint that adds to the conversation].

[Optional: question that deepens engagement]
```

**Rules:**
- Reference what they actually said (no generic "great point!")
- Add genuine value in your reply
- Keep it under 3 sentences
- Ask a question only if it's natural

### Connection Request Message (2nd/3rd Degree)
When a non-connection engages with your content:

```
[Name], saw your comment on my [topic] post.

[One specific observation about their profile/comment].

Would love to connect — I share [content type] regularly that might be useful for [their role/industry].
```

**Rules:**
- Reference the specific interaction
- Show you looked at their profile (one detail)
- Frame the connection as value for THEM
- Never pitch in a connection request

### Post-Connection DM (After They Accept)
First message after connection is accepted:

```
[Name], thanks for connecting.

Quick question: What's the biggest [relevant challenge] you're dealing with right now in [their area]?

No pitch — genuinely curious. I've been writing about this a lot lately and want to make sure I'm covering what actually matters.
```

**Rules:**
- Thank briefly, don't gush
- Ask ONE specific question
- Explicitly state "no pitch" to lower guard
- Frame it as research/curiosity

### Follow-Up Message (5 Days No Reply)
First follow-up after no response:

```
[Name], just bumping this up.

I know [their role] keeps you busy — totally get it.

[One new piece of value: insight, resource, or relevant observation].

Worth a quick look when you have 2 min.
```

**Rules:**
- Acknowledge they're busy (no guilt)
- Add NEW value — never just "following up"
- Keep the ask tiny ("2 min", "quick look")
- One follow-up only, then stop

### Follow-Up Message (Re-engagement after Silence)
When someone went quiet mid-conversation:

```
[Name], was just [relevant trigger: reading about X, saw news about Y].

Made me think of our conversation about [topic].

[New insight or question].
```

**Rules:**
- Use a natural trigger (not "just checking in")
- Reference the previous conversation specifically
- Bring something new to the table

## Language Guidelines

### Do Use
- "Here's what I'd do..."
- "Most people get this wrong..."
- "The fastest way to..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- Numbers and specifics
- Short, punchy sentences
- Direct questions

### Never Use
- "I hope this message finds you well"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "Let's leverage synergies"
- "Would love to pick your brain"
- "As per my last message"
- Generic compliments ("Love your work!")
- Multiple exclamation marks

### Tone Calibration
- **Comments**: Helpful expert, slightly casual
- **Connection requests**: Respectful peer, curious
- **DMs**: Direct, value-first, no fluff
- **Follow-ups**: Low-pressure, new value added

---

## Quality Checklist

Before sending any message, verify:

- [ ] Opens with value or specific reference (not a pitch)
- [ ] Contains at least one specific detail (name, number, reference)
- [ ] Under 4 sentences (for comments) or 5 sentences (for DMs)
- [ ] No corporate buzzwords
- [ ] Has a clear next step or open question
- [ ] Would YOU respond to this message?
- [ ] Personalized — could NOT be sent to anyone else

---

## Anti-Patterns

1. **The Spray-and-Pray**: Same message to everyone → always personalize
2. **The Novel**: Messages longer than a phone screen → cut in half, then cut again
3. **The Humble Brag**: "We just hit $10M..." → only share results that help THEM
4. **The Fake Question**: "Have you thought about using [my product]?" → ask real questions
5. **The Follow-Up Nag**: 3+ follow-ups → one follow-up with new value, then stop
